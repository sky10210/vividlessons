Financial Planning & Goal Setting

A plain teacher-facing guide for the Vivid Lessons interactive module. This guide is designed for planning, answer checking, reflection review, and classroom use.

Overview

Module Purpose

Students determine the financial impact of long-term goals and apply a systematic decision-making process, including opportunity costs, to setting and achieving financial goals.

Best Use Cases

  • Personal finance goals unit
  • Financial planning introduction
  • Sub plan or independent activity
  • Career, education, lifestyle, and savings planning
  • Review before a budgeting or financial decision-making assessment
Learning Goals

Students Should Be Able To

  • Define short-term, intermediate, and long-term financial goals.
  • Explain how long-term goals affect education, lifestyle, family, and future costs.
  • Write stronger SMART financial goals.
  • Identify opportunity cost in realistic decisions.
  • Apply a decision-making process to compare options.
  • Calculate a monthly savings target.

Student Fields Collected

First name, last name, class period, answers, score, completion status, timing, and reflection.

Standards Alignment

Grades 9–12 Personal Finance Fundamentals

17.1.9-12.A: Determine the financial impact of various long-term goals, including lifestyle, family, and education. 17.1.9-12.B: Apply a systematic decision-making process, including opportunity costs, to setting and achieving financial goals.

Answer Key

Question-by-Question Guide

1 — Financial Goal DefinitionCorrect: Save $900 for car insurance by August. The goal is money-related, specific, and time-bound.
2 — Long-Term GoalCorrect: Planning how to pay for education or training after high school.
3 — Financial ImpactCorrect: Ask about income, upfront costs, monthly bills, and emergency money needed for the apartment goal.
4 — SMART GoalCorrect: Save $50 per month for 8 months to pay certification exam fees.
5 — Opportunity CostCorrect: Buying the phone gives up or delays car savings progress.
6 — Decision-Making ProcessCorrect: Define the goal and collect cost, income, and timeline information for each option.
7 — MatchingFinancial Goal = money-related target with a purpose; Opportunity Cost = next best option given up; Trade-Off = comparing benefits and costs between options; Action Step = specific behavior that moves the plan forward.
8 — SortingCorrect order: Define the goal clearly → Estimate cost, timeline, and opportunity cost → Choose action steps and a savings amount → Track progress and adjust.
9 — Mistake CorrectionCorrect: Revise to a measurable plan, such as saving $600 by graduation by putting $30 from each paycheck into savings.
10 — CalculationCorrect: $1,200 ÷ 8 months = $150 per month.
11 — Compare Two ChoicesCorrect: Compare career goal, budget, timeline, and likely earnings.
12 — Real-World DecisionCorrect: Check how the trip affects the goal, adjust the timeline if needed, and choose a realistic amount to spend.
13 — ReviewCorrect: Goal, cost, timeline, and action steps.
14 — Final ApplicationCorrect: The exam plan with $450 cost, $75 monthly savings for 6 months, deadline comparison, and avoiding using the money elsewhere.
15 — Opportunity Cost in Goal SettingCorrect: Opportunity cost helps compare what you gain with what you give up when choosing a goal path.
Reflection Guidance

What to Look For

Students should explain one thing they learned and connect it to a real-world goal. Strong reflections mention a specific goal, cost, timeline, trade-off, or opportunity cost.

Common Misconceptions

  • Thinking a goal is strong just because it sounds positive.
  • Ignoring opportunity cost because the purchase seems small.
  • Assuming the cheapest option is always the best option.
  • Forgetting that long-term goals can affect income, debt, and lifestyle.
Suggested Use

Classroom Flow

  • Launch as a 10–15 minute individual activity.
  • Use as a sub plan, lesson check, or personal finance warm-up extension.
  • After completion, ask students to share one goal and one opportunity cost.
  • Collect certificates or completion screenshots if not connected to a dashboard.
